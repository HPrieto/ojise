The Dispossesed African Child

You are from over there and do not understand

Posted by Donatus Olukanni on August 30, 2016

This is to express my kudos to the young man organizing this event in Ibadan. Please encourage him and others that reasons like him. The idea of "Omidan Yoruba Beauty pagent" is awesome and I hope it takes off all over Yoruba land. It is a women's right issue, when our ladies understand that they dont have to be subjected to "rags and half nudity", before the society percieves them as beautiful.

I believe no culture has a broader spectrum of feminine beauty on the planet, the way Yoruba does. Our women should also avoid this bleaching craze. Black is beautiful. Absolutely!

Often, I encounter people on the continent who attempts to silence or invalidate the opinion of diaspora Africans, simply because "you are from over there" and "you do not understand".

There was even a time I was privied to one woman who works in ECOWAS at Abuja, accusing African diasporas as "people who pretends to care about Africa".

She believes the only people who truly cares are the ones "in the trenches", quoting her verbatim.

The individual reminds me of a line in one of Wole Soyinkas poem, "the ripest fruit is the saddest", considering thus ECOWAS lady was well educated in Europe before ending up in West Africa.

My rumimation is thus: When money is sent from abroad to pay school fees, the diaspora is not accused of lacking understanding or simcerity.

When someone gets sick and get money, there is no acusation of not understanding the need for healthcare, in a system that does not provide it for its citizens.

The plumbers, carpenters,.bricklayers will not refuse work from a diasporan. Diaspora establishes businesses and employs workers, they are not accused of lacking the umderstanding that there is need for jobs on the continent.

But the moment that these same diasporan African becomes critical of a degenerate polity, then it is because they do not understand.
